## 📖 Project Overview 
Developed a comprehensive Business Intelligence solution to evaluate business performance, customer behaviour, product profitability and retention opportunities for a national office supplies retailer - Office Essentials Australia (OEA).

## 📖 Project Highlights
| Area | Project|
| ------------- | ------------- |
| Data Understanding | Profiles six years of B2B transactional data, customer records, product information, sales performance and regional business activity across Australia  |
| Data Preparation  | Perform ETL and data wrangling by using Tableau Prep Builder, eliminating nulls, duplicated values and check spellings  |
| Churn Analysis | Measures customer lifetime value, retention patterns, cohort performance and churn risk indicators  |
| RFM Modelling | Classify customers by recency, frequency and monetary value  |
| Tableau Delivery | Delivers interactive dashboards  |

## 📶 Dataset Snapshot
Using 6-year (2019 to 2024) transactional dataset of 125k records 
Data Model:
- Transaction Table
- Product Table
- Client Table

## 📊 Analytical Themes
**1. Business Performance & Regional Growth Analysis**

Evaluated overall business health through KPI monitoring, profit trend analysis, regional performance benchmarking, forecasting, and scenario modelling. Identified growth disparities across Australian states and assessed market expansion opportunities.

**2. Customer Segmentation & Profitability Analytics**

Applied RFM segmentation and profitability analysis to classify customers based on purchasing behaviour, frequency, recency, and monetary value. Identified high-value customer groups and opportunities for retention, upselling, and resource optimization.

**3. Product Performance & Margin Optimization**

Analyzed product sales, margin contribution, demand elasticity, and category performance to determine profitability drivers and underperforming product segments. Developed recommendations for pricing, bundling, and inventory strategies.

**4. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) & Churn Analytics**

Investigated customer lifetime value trends, churn patterns, and retention risks across regions. Combined cohort analysis and churn metrics to support proactive customer retention initiatives and long-term revenue growth.

**5. Strategic Decision Support & What-if scenario Analysis**

Developed data-driven recommendations using forecasting, what-if simulations, and customer behavior insights to support strategic planning and sustainable business growth.
  
## 📌 Limitations
- Analysis was based solely on historical transactional data and did not incorporate external market, competitor, or economic indicators.
- Customer demographic and behavioral attributes were limited, restricting deeper predictive modeling and personalization opportunities.
- Churn analysis relied on historical inactivity thresholds rather than machine learning-based prediction models.
- Product demand forecasting and scenario simulations assumed relatively stable market conditions and may not fully reflect future economic changes.
- The dataset represented a single organization, limiting generalizability across industries or markets.

## ✔️ Future Improvements
- Develop predictive machine learning models for customer churn, CLV forecasting, and demand prediction.
- Integrate external datasets such as economic indicators, market trends, competitor data, and industry benchmarks.
- Implement automated real-time dashboard refreshes and monitoring through cloud-based data pipelines.
- Enhance customer segmentation using clustering techniques and behavioral analytics beyond traditional RFM analysis.
- Introduce advanced pricing optimization models and recommendation systems to improve cross-selling, upselling, and profitability.
- Expand the solution with geospatial analytics and predictive forecasting to support strategic market expansion decisions.

## 🔍 Key Insights Summary
**1. Business Performance Overview**
2024 profit reached $2.33M, up 4.6% YoY. Revenue shows clear seasonality, peaking in Q4 (October) driven by procurement cycles and year-end budget utilisation. Furniture and Technology categories remain below target, indicating structural underperformance.

**2. Regional Performance**
WA and Queensland are key growth engines. NSW and Victoria are high-volume but mature markets with limited expansion. ACT and NT show early-stage growth potential. Tasmania and South Australia underperform across both revenue and growth. At city level, smaller markets (e.g., Albury, Fremantle) outperform major metros such as Sydney and Brisbane.

**3. Customer Segmentation (RFM)**
Six segments identified: Champions, Loyal, Promising, New, Need Attention, and Lost. Loyal and Promising customers account for ~50% of the base and contribute $48M+ in sales. Champions deliver highest value per transaction, while Need Attention and Lost segments represent elevated churn exposure.

**4. Customer Profitability (Pareto)**
Revenue follows a strong 80/20 distribution, with the top 20% of customers generating >80% of profit. Key high-value accounts include Quality First Services, Twin Peaks, and Highway Heroes. The bottom 5% of customers are consistently unprofitable despite repeat purchasing.

**5. Product Performance**
Paper is the primary profit driver with strong volume and margins. Chairs and Phones generate high revenue but low margins. Accessories, Tables, and Fasteners present high-margin but low-volume opportunities suitable for bundling. Machines and Copiers show sustained decline, consistent with digital transformation and remote work adoption.

**6. Price Elasticity & Demand**
Technology products are highly price elastic due to strong substitutes. Office Supplies are inelastic, reflecting essential demand. Furniture exhibits moderate elasticity due to infrequent, considered purchase behaviour.

**7. Customer Value & Retention**
Average CLV is $24,951 with a ~40-month lifespan, increasing from $24K (2020) to $29K (Q1 2025). Churn is highest in Victoria (57%), Tasmania (50%), and WA (46%), while Queensland records the strongest retention at 11%. Cohort analysis shows churn risk emerging between Quarters 5-8.
